The GeoID Initiative

World‑leading geohazard forecast application that puts your safety in your own hands

Using global weather forecasts, GeoID estimates how the Earth’s surface responds—combining satellite data, best‑practice geomechanics, and AI to condense complex maths into a simple Factor of Safety score. The result is a global, open risk index people can act on directly, with historical tracking to challenge unfair quotes and politicised narratives.

Why GeoID

A Broken Early Warning Landscape

Public exposure to geohazard risk is rising, but awareness and control over risk intelligence is not. People still depend on black‑box insurance assessments, delayed alerts, and fragmented systems that rarely reach those most exposed.

GeoID flips that model with a forecasted Factor of Safety signal that is readable, shareable, and actionable. It gives individuals, communities, and responders the autonomy to act without waiting for political permission or insurer‑controlled narratives.

By publishing long‑run tracking and transparent methods, GeoID gives people the evidence to challenge bad quotes and biased assumptions.

How GeoID Works

Turning Earth Science into Early Warnings

GeoID integrates Earth observation data, geomechanical modelling, and machine learning into a unified digital twin framework. The result is a continuously updated Factor of Safety (FoS) index that reflects real‑world instability across multiple hazards and remains available to anyone, anywhere. Methods and assumptions will be published as the project matures.

Forecast-Driven Risk Computation

Capitalising on input weather forecast data from the OpenWeather API, GeoID will calculate the modelled Factor of Safety to anticipate rising instability before it is visible - providing earlier alerts and longer lead times for communities and responders.

Advanced Mathematical Models

GeoID relies on finite element modelling (FEM), which is widely regarded as a best-practice approach for simulating stress, deformation, and failure processes. These models will integrate a variety of geospatial data and are augmented by machine learning to identify emerging instability before visible failure occurs.

Bridging Science and Society

GeoID provides a clear connection between scientists and the public, it provides a universally accessible platform to turn raw monitoring data into immediately actionable information. All that is required is an internet connection and downloading the app.

Our Vision

Democratizing Geohazard Science

Making critical hazard data accessible, portable, and globally distributed—from researchers to the communities at risk.

The GeoID App Experience

The planned app will be designed to make geophysical models readily available for real‑world decisions, for all audiences. A clean map view, locality search, and a single Factor of Safety signal turn complex models into a clear, shareable risk readout.

Users can tap into time‑series views inspired by market charts, compare historical tracks, and see how forecast inputs shift risk ahead of impact. The goal is immediate comprehension without dumbing down the science.

By combining transparent methodology, open data pathways, and global reach, the app democratizes geoscience—giving communities direct access to the same evidence base used by experts.

Programs

From Research to Deployment

GeoID’s programs focus on developing, validating, and deploying real-world early warning capability — from fundamental research to operational pilots.

Pilot Deployment

Ireland & Northern Ireland Pilot

GeoID will be piloted across Northern Ireland and coastal Ireland, integrating landslide, flood, and slope stability risk into a unified early warning framework.

This pilot phase will validate real-world performance, stakeholder usability, and response effectiveness prior to global scaling.

Flood

Flood Risk Tracker

Hydro-meteorological inputs feed the digital twin to surface FoS trends that signal emerging flood risk.

Landslide

Landslides & Slope Instability

We model terrain change and rainfall loading to compute FoS, calculating slope stability and landslide susceptibility.

Get Involved

Building GeoID Is a Collective Effort

GeoID is an open initiative at the intersection of science, technology, and public safety. We welcome collaborators and supporters who share our commitment to transparent, accessible hazard intelligence.

GeoID is in early research and pilot‑proposal stages. Our goal is to be genuinely disruptive to legacy insurance models by making risk intelligence public, auditable, and actionable. Long‑term, GeoID seeks to establish a dedicated research hub that creates pathways for geoscientist graduates who want to make a real difference, with any commercialization only considered after research outcomes are fulfilled.
